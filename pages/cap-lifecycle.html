Chapter 5

The CMMC Assessment Process (CAP) Lifecycle

Home › CAP Lifecycle

The CMMC Assessment Process (CAP) is the procedural guide that C3PAOs must follow to ensure that assessments are thorough, consistent, and defensible. The CAP organizes the assessment lifecycle into four primary phases, each with specific deliverables and decision points.

Phase 1: Planning and Preparation

The assessment journey begins with a "Pre-Assessment" phase where the C3PAO validates that the Organization Seeking Certification (OSC) is genuinely ready for a formal evaluation. This phase involves:

  • High-level review of the OSC's System Security Plan (SSP)
  • Review of asset inventories and network diagrams
  • Finalizing the CMMC Assessment Scope — identifying every system, application, and facility to be evaluated
  • Producing the Pre-Assessment Form documenting the organization's CAGE code and readiness determination

Phase 2: Conducting the Assessment

This is the technical core of the engagement. It begins with an "In-Brief" meeting to align all stakeholders on schedule and procedures. The assessment team evaluates implementation of all 110 security requirements from NIST SP 800-171 Revision 2 using three methods defined in NIST SP 800-171A:

  • Examine: Reviewing artifacts like router configurations, audit logs, and training records
  • Interview: Speaking with system administrators, HR managers, and end-users to ensure policies are followed in practice
  • Test: Observing actual execution of security controls, such as penetration tests or physical access checks
Daily Checkpoints: The C3PAO holds daily checkpoint meetings with the OSC to address challenges or clarify findings in real-time, ensuring transparency throughout the process.

Phase 3: Reporting Results

After evidence collection, the assessment team compiles findings and determines if each practice is "MET" or "NOT MET". Before results are finalized, a formal Quality Assurance review is conducted by a CCA who was not part of the assessment team. The findings are presented to the OSC in an "Out-Brief" meeting before being submitted into the DoD's CMMC instantiation of eMASS.

Phase 4: Certification and POA&M Closeout

The final phase involves formal issuance of the CMMC Level 2 certificate. If the OSC had minor gaps, they might receive a "Conditional Level 2" status, which gives them 180 days to remediate specific "Not Met" practices through a Plan of Action and Milestones (POA&M). The C3PAO must then conduct a "POA&M closeout assessment" to verify remediation before a final, unconditional certificate is granted.
