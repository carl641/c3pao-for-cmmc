Chapter 9

Common Pitfalls and Assessment Failures

Home › Common Pitfalls

C3PAO assessments are rigorous, and failure is a real possibility for organizations that treat compliance as a "check-the-box" exercise. Most failures are not due to lack of security tools, but rather a lack of documentation and operational institutionalization.

The Top Reasons for Assessment Failure

1. Incomplete Documentation

An organization may have an MFA tool active, but if there is no policy governing its use, no procedure for how accounts are managed, and no evidence of training, the C3PAO cannot mark the practice as "Met."

Key Insight: Documentation is not a supplement to technical controls — it IS the evidence. Without written policies, procedures, and logs, even a perfectly secure environment will fail the assessment.

2. Stale System Security Plans (SSP)

The SSP is a living document. If it does not reflect the actual current configuration of the network at the time of the audit, the assessment will likely be paused or failed. Common gaps include outdated network diagrams, missing new systems, and unchanged revision dates.

3. Misidentified CUI Flow

If the C3PAO discovers CUI residing on a system that was marked as "Out-of-Scope," it indicates a fundamental failure in scoping, which can invalidate the entire assessment boundary.

4. Poor External Service Provider (ESP) Management

Many contractors rely on Managed Service Providers (MSPs) for IT. If the MSP does not meet the necessary CMMC or FedRAMP standards for the services they provide, the contractor cannot inherit the compliance, leading to a failure.

5. Failure to Address Assessment Objectives

Each of the 110 controls has multiple "assessment objectives" (totaling 320). Organizations often implement the high-level control but fail to address one of the underlying objectives found in NIST SP 800-171A.

The Role of the "Mock Audit"

To avoid these pitfalls, experts recommend conducting a "Mock Audit" or a formal gap analysis with an RPO before engaging a C3PAO. This serves as a dress rehearsal, allowing the organization to identify and fix vulnerabilities in its body of evidence before the high-stakes, official evaluation begins.

Best Practice: A mock audit typically costs a fraction of the actual C3PAO assessment and can save organizations tens of thousands of dollars by preventing a failed first attempt.
