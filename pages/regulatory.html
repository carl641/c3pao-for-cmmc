Regulatory Frameworks and the Dual Rulemaking Process

Home › Regulatory Frameworks

Two Rules, One Framework

The CMMC program is underpinned by two distinct but complementary regulatory regimes: Title 32 of the Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) and Title 48 CFR. Understanding the interplay between these two rules is critical for any contractor navigating the certification timeline.

32 CFR Part 170 — The "Program Rule"

This rule established the foundational requirements of the CMMC framework, defining the technical levels (1, 2, and 3), scoping guidelines, and the core assessment methodology. Effective in late 2024, it codified the technical "what" of CMMC.

48 CFR — The "Acquisition Rule"

The acquisition rule dictates the "how" and "when" by amending the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement (DFARS) to include CMMC requirements in actual contracts. Contractual enforcement began once the 48 CFR rule was finalized in late 2025.

Phased Implementation Timeline

The DoD has implemented a four-phase rollout over three years to prevent overwhelming the C3PAO community and to allow the DIB time to achieve audit readiness.

1

Phase 1 — Nov 10, 2025 to Nov 9, 2026

Focus on self-assessments; C3PAO audits at DoD discretion.

2

Phase 2 — Nov 10, 2026 to Nov 9, 2027

C3PAO Level 2 certification becomes a condition for contract award in applicable solicitations.

3

Phase 3 — Nov 10, 2027 to Nov 9, 2028

Certification required for award and option periods for Phase 1 awards.

4

Phase 4 — Nov 10, 2028 and Beyond

Full implementation across all applicable solicitations.
