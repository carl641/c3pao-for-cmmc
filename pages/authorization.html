The Path to Authorization for C3PAOs

Home › Authorization

Becoming an authorized C3PAO is an arduous process that subjects the applicant organization to the same level of scrutiny they will eventually apply to their clients. This rigor ensures that entities responsible for verifying national security standards are themselves beyond reproach.

Technical and Security Pre-requisites

A candidate C3PAO must first clear an organizational background check and a Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence (FOCI) review conducted by the Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency (DCSA). This review is critical for ensuring that the C3PAO is not influenced by foreign adversaries, given the sensitive nature of the assessment reports they will generate and handle.

Because C3PAOs store and process assessment data of defense contractors, they are required to meet CMMC Level 2 standards at minimum, and often Level 3 requirements for their own internal systems.

The DIBCAC Assessment

The most significant hurdle is the DIBCAC assessment. To be fully authorized, the candidate C3PAO must pass a Level 2 assessment conducted by the Defense Contract Management Agency's Defense Industrial Base Cybersecurity Assessment Center (DIBCAC). This assessment ensures the C3PAO has fully implemented all 110 controls of NIST SP 800-171. Only after passing this government-led review can the organization be listed as "Authorized" on the Cyber AB Marketplace.

Personnel Certification and Staffing Requirements

A C3PAO is only as strong as its certified staff. The Cyber AB requires each C3PAO to maintain an association with at least:

  • One Lead CMMC Certified Assessor (LCCA)
  • One CMMC Certified Assessor (CCA)
  • A Quality Assurance individual who is also a CCA
  • Up to three authorized certifying officials empowered to sign and issue certificates

The certification of these individuals involves a separate, rigorous pathway overseen by the CMMC Assessor and Instructor Certification Organization (CAICO). Candidates must pass professional examinations and achieve a favorable Tier 3 background investigation determination, equivalent to what is required for national security eligibility.
